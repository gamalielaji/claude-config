## Next-Generation Non-Stimulants

### Centanafadine (Otsuka) — FDA Decision July 24, 2026
- **Class:** First-in-class NDSRI (norepinephrine, dopamine, serotonin reuptake inhibitor)
- **Mechanism:** Strongest affinity for NE transporters, then DA, then 5-HT
- **Key data:** 4 Phase 3 trials (children, adolescents, 2x adults). High-dose arms showed significant improvement. 50% of adult patients achieved ≥50% symptom reduction. Separated from placebo within 1 week.
- **Side effects:** Decreased appetite, headache (adults); decreased appetite, nausea, rash (pediatric). Low abuse potential.
- **Executive function:** Tim Wilens (Harvard/MGH) noted improvements in time management, hierarchical thinking, organization, start/stop/shift transitions.
- **Why it matters:** Triple mechanism may simultaneously sharpen attention (NE/DA) while stabilizing emotion (5-HT). Not a controlled substance. 52-week safety data shows sustained benefit.
- **Limitation:** Effect size smaller than stimulants. Low-dose arms did not reach significance.

### Viloxazine XR (Qelbree, Supernus) — Already FDA-Approved
- **Class:** Selective norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor with serotonin receptor modulation
- **Mechanism:** Increases NE and DA prefrontally (like stimulants) but without CNS stimulation. Low abuse potential, not a controlled substance.
- **APSARD 2026 data:** Real-world clinic data showed significant improvements in cognitive executive functioning AND emotional dysregulation. Improvements in depression and anxiety. Unexpected improvement in sleep. 3-year study in 600 individuals ongoing; 1-year follow-up shows continued efficacy, 6% dropout rate.
- **Executive function (BRIEF-A):** Global Executive Composite -12.4 (P<0.0001), Metacognition -12.6 (P<0.0001), Behavioral Regulation -10.0 (P<0.0001). Improvements continued 24+ months.
- **Side effects (adults):** Insomnia (14.8%), fatigue (11.6%), nausea (10.1%), decreased appetite, dry mouth, headache.
- **Dosing:** 200–600 mg/day. Median effective dose 400mg/day. Once daily, morning or evening.
- **Why it matters:** Improves executive function + emotion + sleep. For founders/operators, this combination is uniquely valuable.

## Smarter Stimulant Delivery

### Azstarys (serdexmethylphenidate/dexmethylphenidate)
- Prodrug + immediate-release combo for smoother onset, extended duration
- Eliminates the chaotic unmedicated morning window
- Lower abuse potential than traditional stimulants

### Arynta (lisdexamfetamine oral solution) — Available Mid-2026
- Same active ingredient as Vyvanse, liquid form
- Duration: 13–14 hours
- Flexible titration via liquid dosing

---

## Combination Strategies

### Stimulant + Alpha-2 Agonist (Guanfacine/Clonidine)
- Stimulant = focus; Alpha-2 agonist = emotional smoothing
- Promising for ADHD + anxiety comorbidity
- Reduces jitters, emotional reactivity, and sleep disruption from stimulants

### Pharmacogenomic Testing
- New tools predict medication response based on genetic profile
- Reduces "try for 3 months, fail, try another" cycle
- Increasingly covered by insurance

## Neuromodulation

### TMS (Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation)
- 2025 meta-analysis (8 RCTs, 325 patients): Large effect sizes for inattention (SMD -0.94) and hyperactivity/impulsivity (SMD -0.98)
- Effects persist at 1-month follow-up
- Deep TMS reaches ~5.5cm depth, targeting deeper cortical structures
- Protocol: 3–6 week course
- Upgrades prefrontal cortex without affecting creative/divergent circuits

### tDCS (Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation)
- Lower-tech, more accessible
- Promising when combined with cognitive training
- Moving toward at-home devices
- Evidence still early (small sample sizes)

### Neurofeedback
- JAMA Psychiatry 2025 meta-analysis (38 RCTs): NOT significant at group level
- Small effect with established protocols (SMR, theta-beta ratio)
- Processing speed only neuropsych outcome with improvement
- Viable for medication-averse patients; don't expect miracles

## The Competitive Advantage Stack

**Layer 1 (Foundation):** Sleep/circadian reset — free, 2–3 weeks
**Layer 2 (Baseline):** Morning exercise — 30–45 min, 4–5x/week
**Layer 3 (Chemistry):** Viloxazine XR or Centanafadine (broad-spectrum, low drawdown) OR stimulant + alpha-2 agonist (max output) OR hybrid (non-stimulant daily + PRN stimulant)
**Layer 4 (Hardware):** TMS course for structural prefrontal improvement
