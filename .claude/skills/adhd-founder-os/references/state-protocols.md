# State Protocols — Full Intervention Playbooks

## Protocol 1: DOPAMINE DEFICIT → Activate + Revenue Anchor

The task doesn't produce enough dopamine to cross the initiation threshold.

1. **Shrink** — "What's the smallest unit you can finish in 10 min?"
2. **Body-first** — "20 jumping jacks or walk around the block. Now." (Research: Hedges' g = 0.55 for inhibitory control after single exercise session)
3. **Novelty injection** — Change location, music, device, posture. ADHD brains produce dopamine in response to environmental novelty.
4. **Revenue anchor** — "Your R1 priority is [X]. That's where the money is. Start there."
5. **2-min micro-start** — "Open the file. Don't work on it. Just stare at it." Zeigarnik effect: incomplete tasks generate their own pull.

After activation → lock into session protocol with context confirmed.

## Protocol 2: HYPERFOCUS MISDIRECTION → Park + Revenue Redirect

Dopamine locked onto wrong target. Don't fight the mechanism; redirect it.

1. **No shame** — "Brain found dopamine. That's the system working. Now we redirect."
2. **Capture & park** — "60 seconds: write where you are. I'll save it. Not lost."
3. **Revenue check** — "Active R1 project is [X]. That's what we're doing now."
4. **Environment reset** — Close all tangent-related tabs/apps. Physical cue removal breaks the dopamine loop.
5. **Scope test** — If tangent IS related to current project: "Is this moving the deliverable toward shipping? If scope creep, it needs its own 5 gates."
6. **Timer contract** — "25 min on the real priority. Then 10 min earned back on the interesting thing."

## Protocol 3: EXECUTIVE OVERLOAD → Dump + Revenue Triage

Working memory maxed. Never "try harder to hold it." Get it OUT.

1. **Brain dump** — "List everything on your mind. Don't organize, don't prioritize. Just dump."
2. **Revenue-rank** — Claude classifies each item: R1/R2/R3/R4/NOT REVENUE.
3. **Triage to 3** — "Top 3 by revenue impact. Everything else: delegate, schedule, or kill."
4. **Sequence serially** — "#1 first. #2 doesn't exist until #1 done." ADHD can't parallel-process executive tasks.
5. **Active project audit** — "You have [N] active projects. If >3, which ships or dies NOW?"
6. **Delegate remainder** — Everything not in top 3 → handoff brief or schedule to specific future date.

Route to `daily-briefing` with triaged list.

## Protocol 4: EMOTIONAL DYSREGULATION → Process Fast + Revenue Redirect

NOT about "calming down." ADHD emotional responses are neurologically 10x. Dismissing them makes it worse.

1. **Name it** — "This is [RSD/frustration/anxiety/anger]. 10x response to 1x stimulus. Neurology, not weakness."
2. **Time-box** — "5 minutes to feel it fully. Timer. When it goes off, we work."
3. **Externalize** — "Tell me exactly what happened and what story your brain is telling you." Saying it out loud often reveals the catastrophization.
4. **Revenue redirect** — "One action that moves R1 forward. Now." Action overrides rumination.
5. **If RSD-specific** — "The rejection is disproportionate to the event. What would you tell a friend describing this exact situation?"

## Protocol 5: ENERGY CRASH → Recovery + Low-Effort Revenue

Don't push through. Neurochemically counterproductive.

1. **Diagnose type:**
   - Medication wearing off → check timing, discuss extended-release with psychiatrist
   - Post-hyperfocus depletion → normal, budget 30-60 min recovery
   - Sleep debt → 20-min power nap (alarm!) or 10-min outdoor walk
   - Circadian dip (2-4pm) → expected, schedule low-cognitive tasks for this window
2. **Physical reset** — "10-min walk outside. Sunlight. Non-negotiable."
3. **Fuel check** — "When did you last eat? Drink water?" ADHD brains forget to eat during hyperfocus.
4. **Strategic surrender** — Switch to easy revenue wins: follow-up emails, CRM updates, simple handoff briefs. Still produces value without deep thinking.
5. **Log crash time** — Track across sessions. Patterns emerge in 7-10 days.

## Protocol 6: DECISION PARALYSIS → Revenue Frame + Constraint

Problem isn't insufficient information. It's insufficient dopamine to commit.

1. **Revenue frame** — "Which option generates revenue faster? That one."
2. **Reversibility check** — Reversible? "Pick the faster one. Speed > optimization."
3. **Two-option max** — If >2: eliminate to 2. ADHD can't compare 5 simultaneously.
4. **Deadline manufacture** — "3 minutes to decide. After that, I pick by revenue impact."
5. **If still stuck** — "Both options are probably fine. Cost of not deciding > cost of picking wrong. Go."

## Protocol 7: TIME BLINDNESS EMERGENCY → Channel Panic + Revenue Priority

Urgency finally crossed the dopamine threshold. Channel it, don't fight it.

1. **Channel** — "Brain has dopamine now. Peak mode. USE IT."
2. **Revenue-prioritize** — Multiple deadlines? Highest revenue impact first.
3. **Minimum viable deliverable** — Strip everything nice-to-have. Ship the core.
4. **30-min checkpoints** — "Check in every 30 min. What shipped?"
5. **Single-tab mode** — Close everything except the ONE thing. Phone on airplane mode.
6. **Post-crisis** — "What system failure caused this? How to build an earlier warning?" Route to `weekly-prep-brief`.
