# IP Enforcement — Global Guide

## 1. Enforcement Pathways

### 1.1 Pre-Litigation

**Cease & Desist Letter**
- First step for most IP disputes
- Cost: $500-5,000 (attorney drafting)
- Timeline: send, allow 10-30 days for response
- Effectiveness: resolves 30-50% of disputes without litigation
- Risk: may trigger declaratory judgment action (US — recipient sues for non-infringement)
- Best practice: professional, factual, specific about the infringement and requested relief

**Negotiation / Settlement**
- Most IP disputes settle before trial (US: ~95% of patent cases settle)
- Common outcomes: license agreement, coexistence agreement, phase-out period
- Mediation: voluntary, facilitated negotiation. WIPO Mediation available

**Administrative Proceedings**
- Trademark opposition: file during publication period (typically 2-3 months)
- Patent post-grant review: challenge validity at patent office
  - US: Inter Partes Review (IPR) at PTAB — 12-18 months, ~$300K-500K
  - EU: Opposition at EPO — 9-18 months
  - China: Patent Invalidation at CNIPA — 6-12 months
- Trademark cancellation: challenge existing registration (non-use, bad faith, generic)
- Domain name disputes: UDRP at WIPO — $1,500-5,000, 60-90 days

### 1.2 Civil Litigation

**Forum Selection Considerations:**
| Factor | US | EU/UPC | UK | China | Japan |
|--------|----|----|----|----|-----|
| **Speed to trial** | 1-3 years | 1-2 years (UPC: 12-14 months target) | 1-2 years | 6-18 months | 1-2 years |
| **Cost (patent)** | $1M-10M+ | $200K-2M | $500K-3M | $50K-500K | $200K-1M |
| **Discovery** | Extensive | Limited | Moderate | Limited | Limited |
| **Damages** | High (treble available) | Moderate | Moderate | Increasing (punitive up to 5x) | Moderate |
| **Injunction likelihood** | Moderate (eBay test) | High | High | High | High |
| **Jury trial** | Available | No | No | No | No |
| **Bifurcation** | No | Germany: yes. UPC: no | No | No | No |

**US-Specific:**
- Federal courts have exclusive jurisdiction for patents and copyrights
- Trademark: federal (Lanham Act) and state
- ITC Section 337: border exclusion orders for infringing imports. Fast (12-18 months). No damages but powerful injunctive relief
- PTAB IPR: invalidity challenge. ~$300-500K. 12-18 months. Kills ~70% of challenged claims
- Venue: Eastern District of Texas, Western District of Texas (Waco), District of Delaware historically popular for patent plaintiffs. 2017 TC Heartland decision limited venue options

**EU/UPC-Specific:**
- UPC: single court for Unitary Patents and classic European patents (unless opted out)
- Preliminary injunctions available within weeks
- Central Division (Paris + Munich), Local/Regional Divisions
- Opt-out: patent holders can opt classic EPs out of UPC jurisdiction until 2030
- Cost: more predictable than US. Loser-pays principle

**China-Specific:**
- IP courts in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou (2014)
- Supreme People's Court IP Tribunal (2019) — appellate jurisdiction nationwide
- Internet courts: Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou — online disputes
- Speed: among fastest in the world for IP cases (6-18 months)
- Damages: historically low, now increasing dramatically
  - 2025: punitive damages in 30 cases (66.7% YoY increase)
  - Average: 38M RMB per punitive damages case
  - Statutory damages cap: 5M RMB (copyright), 5M RMB (trademark), 1M RMB (patent)
- Evidence preservation orders available
- Administrative enforcement: SAMR/local AMR can raid, seize, fine without going to court

### 1.4 Border Measures / Customs Enforcement

**How it works:**
1. Record IP rights with customs authority
2. Customs detains suspected infringing goods at border
3. Right holder notified and examines goods
4. If confirmed infringing: goods destroyed. If not: released
5. Cost: registration typically $100-1,000. Destruction costs may be shared

**Key customs programs:**
- **US CBP:** Recordation with CBP. Free. Extremely effective for counterfeits
- **EU Customs Regulation (608/2013):** Single application covers all EU borders
- **China Customs:** Record with GAC. Active seizure and destruction program
- **Japan Customs:** Recordation system. High seizure rates
- **ASEAN:** Varying levels. Singapore and Malaysia most active

## 2. Damages & Remedies

### 2.1 Types of Damages

**Compensatory Damages:**
- **Lost profits:** Actual sales/revenue lost due to infringement. Requires proof of causation (Panduit test in US: demand, absence of alternatives, capacity, profit margin)
- **Reasonable royalty:** Hypothetical negotiation between willing licensor/licensee. Georgia-Pacific factors (US). Minimum floor
- **Unjust enrichment / Account of profits:** Infringer's profits attributable to infringement. Available in most jurisdictions
- **Actual damages:** Direct financial harm proven by right holder

**Enhanced / Punitive Damages:**
| Jurisdiction | Multiplier | Trigger | Key Case/Law |
|-------------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| US (patent) | Up to 3x | Willful infringement | Halo v. Pulse (2016): discretionary, totality of circumstances |
| US (trademark) | Up to 3x | Willful under Lanham Act §35(a) | |
| US (copyright) | Statutory: $750-$150K per work | Available without proving actual damages | Must register before infringement (or within 3 months of publication) |
| China | Up to 5x | Malicious infringement, serious circumstances | All IP laws (since 2020-2021 amendments) |
| UK | "Additional damages" | Flagrancy, benefit to infringer | CDPA s.97(2) |
| France | "Triple damages" | Since 2014 reform | |
| Japan | Not available | — | But courts increasingly awarding higher base damages |
| Germany | License analogy | Lost profits OR license analogy OR disgorgement | Three-track system |

**Statutory Damages (no proof of actual harm needed):**
| Jurisdiction | Copyright | Trademark | Patent |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| US | $750-$30K per work ($150K willful) | Actual damages or profits | No statutory damages |
| China | Up to 5M RMB | Up to 5M RMB | Up to 1M RMB (increasing via amendments) |
| Canada | $500-$20K per work (commercial); $100-$5K (non-commercial) | N/A | N/A |
| Indonesia | Criminal fines up to 4B IDR | Criminal fines | Criminal fines |

**Injunctive Relief:**
- **Preliminary injunction:** Before trial. Must show likelihood of success, irreparable harm, balance of hardships, public interest (US: eBay factors)
- **Permanent injunction:** After trial. Standard remedy in most countries (but not automatic in US since eBay v. MercExchange 2006)
- **Seizure orders:** Emergency measures. Available in most jurisdictions. Anton Piller order (UK/Commonwealth), saisie-contrefaçon (France)
- **Destruction/recall:** Court orders infringing goods destroyed or recalled from market

### 2.2 Attorney's Fees

| Jurisdiction | Rule | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| US | American Rule (each side pays own). Exception: "exceptional case" | Patent: §285 (exceptional case = fee shifting). Trademark: similar. Copyright: §505 |
| UK/EU | English Rule (loser pays) | Cost recovery typically 60-80% of actual costs |
| China | Winner can recover "reasonable expenses" | Not full indemnity but growing |

## 3. Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

### 3.1 WIPO Arbitration & Mediation Center

**Services:**
- Mediation: voluntary, facilitated negotiation. $200/hour (mediator fee)
- Arbitration: binding decision. Full evidentiary hearing. $300-600/hour (arbitrator)
- Expedited arbitration: streamlined, faster, cheaper
- Expert determination: technical issues decided by expert (not legal determination)

**2025 Statistics:**
- 1,461 disputes handled (70% YoY increase)
- Copyright/digital content: 71% of cases
- Trademark: 23%
- Patent: 6%
- Parties from 70+ countries
- Shanghai service: 200+ cases in 2025

**Advantages over litigation:**
- Single proceeding (vs. multi-jurisdiction litigation)
- Confidential (no public record)
- Faster (6-12 months typical)
- Expert decision-makers (IP specialists)
- Enforceable worldwide (New York Convention for arbitral awards)

### 3.2 UDRP (Uniform Domain-Name Dispute-Resolution Policy)

**What it covers:** Domain name disputes (cybersquatting)
**Filed at:** WIPO Arbitration Center (or other providers: NAF, ADNDRC, CAC)
**Requirements (all three must be met):**
1. Domain name identical or confusingly similar to complainant's trademark
2. Registrant has no rights or legitimate interests in the domain
3. Domain registered and used in bad faith

**Cost:** $1,500 (single panelist) to $5,000 (3-member panel)
**Timeline:** ~60-90 days from filing to decision
**Remedies:** Transfer or cancellation of domain name (no damages)
**Statistics:** WIPO handled 6,000+ UDRP cases in 2024. Complainant success rate: ~85%

### 3.3 URS (Uniform Rapid Suspension)

- Faster/cheaper alternative to UDRP for clear-cut cybersquatting
- Only available for new gTLDs (not .com, .net, .org)
- Standard of proof: "clear and convincing evidence"
- Result: suspension (not transfer)
- Cost: ~$375-$500
- Timeline: ~30 days

## 4. Platform-Specific Enforcement

### 4.1 DMCA Notice-and-Takedown (US)

**Process:**
1. Identify infringing content on platform
2. Send DMCA takedown notice to platform's designated agent
3. Platform must "expeditiously" remove/disable access
4. Alleged infringer can file counter-notification
5. If counter-notification filed: content restored in 10-14 business days unless right holder files lawsuit

**Requirements for valid notice (§512(c)(3)):**
- Physical/electronic signature of right holder
- Identification of copyrighted work
- Identification of infringing material (specific URL)
- Contact information
- Good faith statement
- Statement under penalty of perjury that you're authorized

**Abuse risk:** Wrongful DMCA takedowns can result in liability (Lenz v. Universal: must consider fair use before filing)

### 4.2 EU Digital Services Act (DSA)

- Platforms must have notice-and-action mechanisms for illegal content (including IP infringement)
- "Trusted flaggers": recognized entities whose notices get priority processing
- Transparency reporting requirements
- Very large platforms (VLOPs): enhanced obligations (systemic risk assessment, compliance officers)
- Penalties: up to 6% of global turnover

### 4.3 Platform-Specific Programs

| Platform | Program | IP Types | Process |
|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| Amazon | Brand Registry + Project Zero | Trademark, copyright, patent | Automated protection + manual reporting |
| Google/YouTube | Content ID | Copyright (audio/video) | Automated fingerprinting. Monetize, block, or track |
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | IP Reporting Tool + Rights Manager | Trademark, copyright | Manual reporting + automated for video/images |
| TikTok | IP Infringement Report | Copyright, trademark | Manual reporting form |

## 5. Anti-Counterfeiting Strategy

### 5.1 Physical Products

**Prevention:**
- Tamper-evident packaging
- Holograms, QR codes, serialization
- Blockchain-based authentication
- Distribution channel control
- Authorized dealer agreements

**Detection:**
- Customs recordation (border measures)
- Market surveillance (online + physical)
- Test purchases (mystery shopping)
- Consumer reporting mechanisms

**Enforcement:**
- Customs seizure and destruction
- Civil litigation (damages + injunction)
- Criminal prosecution (large-scale operations)
- Administrative raids (China SAMR, EU customs)

### 5.2 Online Counterfeiting

**Prevention:**
- Domain monitoring and cybersquatting defense
- Social media brand monitoring
- Marketplace brand enrollment (Amazon Brand Registry, etc.)
- Proactive content identification (YouTube Content ID)

**Enforcement:**
- DMCA/DSA takedown notices
- Marketplace IP complaint systems
- UDRP for domain names
- Search engine delisting requests
- Payment processor complaints (cut off revenue)
- ISP blocking orders (certain jurisdictions)

## 6. IP Insurance

### 6.1 Types

**Defense cost insurance (DCI):**
- Covers legal costs of defending against IP infringement claims
- Premiums: $5,000-$50,000/year depending on coverage and risk
- Deductibles: $10,000-$100,000

**Enforcement/pursuit insurance:**
- Covers costs of pursuing infringers
- Less common, more expensive
- Some policies cover lost revenue during enforcement

**Comprehensive IP insurance:**
- Combined defense + enforcement
- May include coverage for damages awards
- Premiums: $10,000-$100,000+/year

### 6.2 When IP Insurance Makes Sense

- Patent-heavy businesses (especially SMEs facing NPE/troll threats)
- Companies entering new markets where infringement risk is unclear
- IP-dependent businesses (brand value, technology licensing)
- As part of M&A due diligence requirements
- Required by some licensing agreements
