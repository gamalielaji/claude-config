# IP Law — Americas

## United States

### Governing Bodies
- **USPTO** (United States Patent and Trademark Office) — patents, trademarks
- **US Copyright Office** (Library of Congress) — copyright registration
- **ITC** (International Trade Commission) — Section 337 investigations (IP at border)
- **DOJ/FBI** — criminal IP enforcement

### Key Statutes
| Area | Statute | Citation |
|------|---------|----------|
| Copyright | Copyright Act | 17 U.S.C. §§101-1332 |
| Trademark | Lanham Act | 15 U.S.C. §§1051-1141 |
| Patent | Patent Act | 35 U.S.C. §§1-376 |
| Trade Secret | Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA) | 18 U.S.C. §§1831-1839 |
| Trade Secret (state) | Uniform Trade Secrets Act (UTSA) | Adopted in 48 states + DC |
| Design Patent | Patent Act Chapter 16 | 35 U.S.C. §§171-173 |
| Semiconductor | Semiconductor Chip Protection Act | 17 U.S.C. Chapter 9 |
| Right of Publicity | State laws (no federal statute) | Varies by state |

### Copyright Specifics
- **Registration:** Not required for protection but required to sue in federal court (for US works). Required for statutory damages ($750-$30,000 per work; up to $150,000 for willful)
- **Work for hire:** Two categories: (1) works by employees in scope of employment; (2) certain commissioned works with written agreement (9 enumerated categories only)
- **Fair use (§107):** Four-factor balancing test. Transformative use heavily favored since Google v. Oracle (2021)
- **DMCA (§512):** Safe harbor for online platforms. Notice-and-takedown system. Counter-notification process
- **Duration:** Life+70 (individual); 95 from publication or 120 from creation (WFH/anonymous/pseudonymous)
- **AI Policy (2025-2026):** Human authorship required. Copyright Office report (May 2025) said AI training fair use is case-by-case. Thaler v. Perlmutter certiorari denied (March 2026)

### Trademark Specifics
- **Registration:** Federal (USPTO) + state. Federal registration gives nationwide constructive notice, presumption of validity, access to federal courts
- **Use requirement:** Must be in "use in commerce" for registration. Statement of Use required. Specimen of use required
- **Intent-to-Use (ITU):** Can file based on bona fide intent. Must prove actual use before registration (up to 36 months of extensions)
- **Supplemental Register:** For descriptive marks acquiring distinctiveness. No presumption of validity but blocks identical marks
- **Section 2(d):** Likelihood of confusion — primary ground for refusal. DuPont factors test
- **Dilution (§43(c)):** Famous marks can prevent dilution by blurring or tarnishment regardless of confusion
- **Cancellation:** Petition at TTAB. Fraud, abandonment (3 years non-use = prima facie), genericness
- **Costs:** $250-$350 per class (TEAS Plus/Standard). Total: $1,500-2,500 with attorney
- **Timeline:** ~8-12 months typical. USPTO targeting 4.5 months to first office action by 2028

### Patent Specifics
- **Types:** Utility (20 years), Design (15 years), Plant (20 years)
- **Patentability:** Novel (§102), Non-obvious (§103), Useful, Eligible subject matter (§101)
- **Section 101 (Alice/Mayo):** Abstract ideas, laws of nature, natural phenomena excluded. Two-step test: (1) directed to abstract idea? (2) inventive concept beyond the abstract idea? Under Director Squires (2026), USPTO signaling more inclusivity for AI/biotech
- **Prosecution:** Average 2-3 years to grant. Cost: $5,000-15,000+ per application
- **Post-grant proceedings:** Inter partes review (IPR) at PTAB — invalidation challenge, faster/cheaper than litigation
- **Damages:** Lost profits, reasonable royalty, enhanced damages (up to 3x for willful, per Halo v. Pulse 2016)

### Trade Secret Specifics
- **DTSA (2016):** Federal civil cause of action. Ex parte seizure in extraordinary circumstances
- **Reasonable measures:** Written policies, NDAs, access controls, exit interviews, encryption
- **Remedies:** Injunction, damages (actual loss + unjust enrichment), exemplary damages (up to 2x for willful)
- **Inevitable disclosure doctrine:** Some courts enjoin employees from working for competitors. Controversial, limited

### 2026 Trends (US)
- USPTO cracking down on fraud/fraudulent specimens in trademark applications
- AI patent eligibility guidance becoming more permissive under Director Squires
- Trade secrets under pressure from AI reverse-engineering capabilities
- Patent trolls/NPEs still active; IPR at PTAB remains key defense
- 47% of practitioners cite unpredictable court outcomes as top concern

## Canada

### Key Statutes
- **Copyright Act** (R.S.C., 1985, c. C-42) — life+70 years (extended from life+50 in 2022)
- **Trade-marks Act** (R.S.C., 1985, c. T-13) — 10-year terms, renewable
- **Patent Act** (R.S.C., 1985, c. P-4) — 20 years from filing
- **Industrial Design Act** (R.S.C., 1985, c. I-9) — 15 years from registration

### Notable Features
- No registration required for trademarks (common law rights exist) but registration strongly recommended
- Fair dealing (not fair use): research, private study, education, parody, satire, criticism, news reporting
- No utility model system
- Strong trade secret protection through common law (breach of confidence)
- CIPO (Canadian Intellectual Property Office) handles all registrations
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague

## Brazil

### Key Statutes
- **Industrial Property Law (9.279/1996)** — patents, trademarks, industrial designs, trade secrets
- **Copyright Law (9.610/1998)** — copyright and neighboring rights
- **Software Law (9.609/1998)** — software protection (copyright-based)

### Notable Features
- **INPI** (National Institute of Industrial Property) — registration authority
- Patent backlog historically severe (improving). Average pendency: 3-5 years
- Prior consent from ANVISA (health regulatory) required for pharmaceutical patents — controversial
- Trademarks: 10-year terms, renewable. First-to-file system
- Pipeline patents abolished. SPC-like extensions not available
- Business methods and software "per se" not patentable (but software with technical effect may be)
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2019)
- NOT member: Hague

## Mexico

### Key Statutes
- **Federal Law for the Protection of Industrial Property (2020)** — comprehensive reform replacing 1991 law
- **Federal Copyright Law**

### Notable Features
- IMPI (Mexican Institute of Industrial Property) — registration authority
- 2020 reform: introduced utility models, industrial secrets protection, expanded trademark types
- Trademarks: 10-year terms. Declaration of use required at year 3
- Patents: 20 years from filing. No patent term adjustment/extension
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague
- USMCA (NAFTA successor) has strong IP chapter (Chapter 20)

## Argentina

### Key Statutes
- **Trademark Law 22.362** — 10-year terms, renewable
- **Patent Law 24.481 (amended by 24.572)** — 20 years from filing
- **Copyright Law 11.723** — life+70 years

### Notable Features
- INPI Argentina handles registrations
- Use requirement for trademarks: must use within 5 years
- Software protected by copyright, not patents
- NOT a PCT member (one of few major economies outside PCT)
- Member: Paris, Berne, Madrid
- NOT member: Hague, PCT
