# IP Law — Asia-Pacific

## China

### Governing Bodies
- **CNIPA** (China National Intellectual Property Administration) — patents, trademarks, GIs
- **NCAC** (National Copyright Administration of China) — copyright
- **SAMR** (State Administration for Market Regulation) — enforcement, anti-counterfeiting

### Key Statutes
| Area | Law | Latest Amendment | Key Provisions |
|------|-----|-----------------|----------------|
| Trademark | Trademark Law | 2019 (major revision pending 2025-2026) | Bad faith filing crackdown, punitive damages up to 5x |
| Patent | Patent Law | 4th amendment 2020 | Patent term extension (pharma), open licensing, punitive damages |
| Copyright | Copyright Law | 3rd amendment 2020 | Statutory damages raised to 5M RMB, punitive damages, broadcast rights |
| Trade Secret | Anti-Unfair Competition Law | 2019 | Administrative enforcement (fines up to 5M RMB, effective June 2026) |
| Design | Patent Law (covers designs) | 2020 | 15-year term (up from 10). Partial design protection |

### China-Specific Features
- **First-to-file (trademarks):** No use requirement for registration. Leads to rampant trademark squatting
- **Bad faith filing:** 427,000 malicious trademark registrations cracked down in 2025. New Trademark Law revision (2025-2026) specifically targets this
- **Utility model:** Very popular. Lower threshold than invention patent. 10 years. ~3M filings/year
- **Punitive damages:** Up to 5x for willful infringement (trademark, patent, copyright, trade secret). 30 cases awarded punitive damages in 2025, averaging 38M RMB per case
- **Specialized IP courts:** Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou IP Courts + Supreme People's Court IP Tribunal (established 2019)
- **Internet courts:** Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou — handle online IP disputes. Beijing court ruled AI-generated image copyrightable (2023) with human intellectual investment
- **Anti-counterfeiting:** Administrative raids (SAMR). Criminal prosecution threshold: 30,000 RMB (counterfeit goods)
- **Patent examination:** 2026 guidelines: ethics reviews for AI, bitstream patent rules, stricter dual filing
- **Agent enforcement:** 8,755 patent agents disbarred in 2025. 180 patent agencies delicensed

### Trademark Registration (China)
- Cost: ~300 RMB (~$42) per class (official fee). Total with attorney: $600-1,600
- Timeline: ~8-12 months (improving from historically 12-18 months)
- Subclass system: China divides Nice classes into subclasses. Must file per subclass
- CRITICAL: File before entering market. Trademark squatters will file first

### Practical Advice for China
1. File trademarks in Chinese characters AND English/Latin script
2. File in all relevant subclasses (broader than Nice classes elsewhere)
3. Monitor for squatters — use CNIPA's opposition/invalidation system
4. Record with customs for border enforcement
5. Build evidence of use and reputation in China specifically

## Japan

### Governing Body
- **JPO** (Japan Patent Office) — patents, utility models, designs, trademarks
- **Agency for Cultural Affairs** — copyright

### Key Statutes
| Area | Law | Duration |
|------|-----|----------|
| Patent | Patent Act (Act No. 121 of 1959, as amended) | 20 years from filing |
| Utility Model | Utility Model Act | 10 years from filing |
| Design | Design Act (amended 2019) | 25 years from filing (up from 20) |
| Trademark | Trademark Act | 10 years, renewable |
| Copyright | Copyright Act (Act No. 48 of 1970, as amended) | Life+70 years |
| Trade Secret | Unfair Competition Prevention Act (UCPA) | N/A (unlimited) |

### Japan-Specific Features
- **Most permissive AI training regime:** Art 30-4 Copyright Act allows reproduction of copyrighted works for AI training unless it "unreasonably harms" copyright holders' interests
- **Design Act reform (2019):** Expanded to cover building interiors, images displayed on screens, non-article-based designs
- **Sound/motion/hologram trademarks:** Available since 2015 reform
- **Accelerated examination:** Super Accelerated Examination — first action within 1 month (patent)
- **IP High Court:** Specialized appellate court (Tokyo) for patent/design/trademark cases
- **Employee inventions:** Employees entitled to "reasonable remuneration" for inventions assigned to employer. Statutory right, cannot be contracted away (but amount can be agreed)
- **Trade secret criminal penalties:** Up to 10 years imprisonment, 20M JPY fine (individual). 1B JPY (corporation)
- **Copyright flex:** Extensive list of exceptions (Art 30-47). Parody exception (Art 30-2, introduced 2018)

## South Korea

### Governing Body
- **KIPO** (Korean Intellectual Property Office)

### Key Statutes
- Patent Act, Utility Model Act, Design Protection Act, Trademark Act, Copyright Act

### Korea-Specific Features
- **6th consecutive year of double-digit patent filing growth** (2024)
- **AI-related patents:** Korea is #2 globally after China in AI patent filings
- **Trade dress protection:** Available under Unfair Competition Prevention Act
- **Punitive damages:** Available for patent infringement (up to 3x, since 2019)
- **Patent prosecution highway (PPH):** Agreements with 30+ countries for accelerated examination
- **Korean IP courts:** Patent Court (Daejeon) handles appeals from KIPO and infringement cases
- **K-content IP:** Strong framework for protecting K-pop, K-drama, webtoon IP
- **Design protection:** 20 years from filing. Covers screen designs, type fonts, interior designs

## India

### Governing Body
- **CGPDTM** (Controller General of Patents, Designs and Trade Marks)
- **Copyright Office** (under DPIIT)

### Key Statutes
| Area | Law | Key Feature |
|------|-----|------------|
| Patent | Patents Act 1970 (amended 2005) | No software/business method patents. Section 3(d): high bar for pharma improvements |
| Trademark | Trade Marks Act 1999 | 10-year terms. Well-known marks register |
| Copyright | Copyright Act 1957 (amended 2012) | Life+60 years. Strong moral rights |
| Design | Designs Act 2000 | 15 years (10+5 extension) |
| Trade Secret | No standalone statute | Common law + contract-based protection |
| GI | Geographical Indications of Goods Act 1999 | Darjeeling tea, Basmati rice, etc. |

### India-Specific Features
- **Section 3(d):** Prevents "evergreening" of pharmaceutical patents. Higher inventiveness bar for new forms of known substances
- **Compulsory licensing:** India has issued compulsory licenses (Natco v. Bayer, 2012 — first and only so far)
- **TKDL (Traditional Knowledge Digital Library):** 300,000+ entries documenting traditional knowledge to prevent misappropriation through patents. Used as prior art to challenge patent applications worldwide
- **Patent backlog:** Improving — 6th consecutive year of double-digit growth in filings
- **No utility model system**
- **Trade secret gap:** No standalone legislation. Protected through NDAs, employment contracts, common law breach of confidence
- **Start-Up IP:** expedited examination for registered startups
- **Member:** Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2013). NOT member: Hague

## Australia

### Governing Body
- **IP Australia** — all IP registrations

### Key Statutes
- Trade Marks Act 1995, Patents Act 1990, Copyright Act 1968, Designs Act 2003

### Australia-Specific Features
- **Innovation patent:** Was unique to Australia (lower threshold, 8 years) — abolished in August 2021. Existing ones valid until expiry
- **Pharmaceutical extensions:** Up to 5 years SPC-like extensions
- **Copyright safe harbor:** Extended to all online service providers (2018, after long delay)
- **Indigenous Knowledge:** Developing sui generis framework for protecting Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander traditional knowledge
- **Member:** Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague

## ASEAN Member States

### Indonesia
- **DJKI** (Direktorat Jenderal Kekayaan Intelektual)
- UU Hak Cipta 28/2014, UU Merek 20/2016, UU Paten 13/2016 (amended by UU 65/2024), UU Desain Industri 31/2000, UU Rahasia Dagang 30/2000
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2018), WCT, WPPT
- NOT member: Hague, Rome Convention
- Trademark cost: ~IDR 1.8M per class (~$115 official fee)
- Patent types: Invention patent (20 years) + Simple patent/utility model (10 years)
- Omnibus UU Cipta Kerja (6/2023) amended several IP laws
- For detailed Indonesian law, use the `indonesian-law` skill with Pasal.id API

### Singapore
- **IPOS** (Intellectual Property Office of Singapore)
- Trade Marks Act (Cap 332), Patents Act (Cap 221), Copyright Act 2021, Registered Designs Act (Cap 266)
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague, WCT, WPPT, Budapest
- IP Hub Master Plan: Singapore positioning as Asia's IP hub
- IPOS International: subsidiary offering IP services regionally
- SG IP FAST: accelerated patent examination (final decision within 6 months)
- Strong enforcement: specialized IP division in High Court
- Copyright Act 2021: major overhaul, introduced computational data analysis exception

### Malaysia
- **MyIPO** (Intellectual Property Corporation of Malaysia)
- Trade Marks Act 2019 (replaced 1976 Act), Patents Act 1983, Copyright Act 1987, Industrial Designs Act 1996
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2019)
- NOT member: Hague
- 2019 Trademark Act: modernized, aligned with Madrid Protocol
- Utility innovation: available (20 years, lower threshold than patent)

### Thailand
- **DIP** (Department of Intellectual Property)
- Trademark Act B.E. 2534 (1991), Patent Act B.E. 2522 (1979), Copyright Act B.E. 2537 (1994)
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2017)
- NOT member: Hague
- Petty patent (utility model): 6+2+2 years (max 10). Lower threshold
- Trade secret: Trade Secrets Act B.E. 2545 (2002)
- Thailand historically on USTR Priority Watch List (improving)

### Philippines
- **IPOPHL** (Intellectual Property Office of the Philippines)
- IP Code (RA 8293, as amended)
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2012)
- NOT member: Hague
- Utility model: 7 years from filing
- Good IP enforcement framework but implementation challenges
- Specialized IP courts

### Vietnam
- **NOIP** (National Office of Intellectual Property)
- IP Law 2005 (amended 2009, 2019, 2022)
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague (since 2019)
- 2022 IP Law amendment: major overhaul aligned with CPTPP and EVFTA obligations
- Utility solution (utility model): 6 years + 2 extensions (max 10)
- Industrial design: 5 years + 2 renewals (max 15)
- Strong trademark squatting risk — file early

### Cambodia, Myanmar, Laos, Brunei
- **Cambodia:** Trademark (2002), Patent (2003), Copyright (2003). Basic IP framework. Joined Madrid (2015)
- **Myanmar:** New Trademark Law (2019), Copyright Law (2019), Patent Law (2019) — still implementing. Joined Madrid (2024)
- **Laos:** IP Law (2011, amended 2017). Joined Madrid (2016). Basic enforcement
- **Brunei:** Patents Order 2011, Trade Marks Act (Cap 98). Small but functional system. PCT-ASPEC participant

## New Zealand
- **IPONZ** (Intellectual Property Office of New Zealand)
- Trade Marks Act 2002, Patents Act 2013, Copyright Act 1994, Designs Act 1953
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague
- Software largely not patentable (Patents Act 2013 s.11)
- Treaty of Waitangi considerations for Maori traditional knowledge
- Copyright: no registration system (automatic protection only)

## Taiwan
- **TIPO** (Taiwan Intellectual Property Office)
- Not a WIPO member (political status). Not a WTO member (but is "Separate Customs Territory of Taiwan, Penghu, Kinmen and Matsu" at WTO)
- Cannot use Madrid System or PCT directly
- Strong IP protection in practice. Specialized IP court since 2008
- Patent: 20 years. Utility model: 10 years. Design patent: 15 years
- Critical manufacturing hub — enforcement matters for supply chain
