# IP Law — Europe

## European Union (Supranational)

### Governing Bodies
- **EUIPO** (European Union Intellectual Property Office, Alicante) — EU trademarks (EUTM) and registered community designs (RCD)
- **EPO** (European Patent Office, Munich) — European patents (separate from EU institutions)
- **UPC** (Unified Patent Court) — operational June 2023, patent litigation for participating EU states
- **National offices** — each member state has its own IP office for national rights

### Key Legal Instruments

**Trademarks:**
- EU Trade Mark Regulation (EU) 2017/1001 (EUTMR)
- EUTM: single filing covers all 27 EU states. 850 EUR (1 class), +50 EUR (2nd class), +150 EUR (3rd+ class)
- 10-year terms, renewable. Use required within 5 years
- Opposition period: 3 months from publication
- Well-known mark protection under Art 8(5) — reputation, unfair advantage, detriment

**Copyright:**
- Copyright Directive (EU) 2019/790 (DSM Directive) — modernized for digital single market
- Art 15: press publishers' right (link tax)
- Art 17: platform liability for user-uploaded content (replaced old safe harbor)
- Art 3-4: text and data mining exceptions (TDM) — critical for AI training. Art 3: research organizations. Art 4: commercial TDM with opt-out
- InfoSoc Directive 2001/29/EC — harmonized copyright exceptions
- Software Directive 2009/24/EC
- Database Directive 96/9/EC — sui generis database right (15 years)
- Duration: life+70 years (harmonized across EU)

**Designs:**
- EU Design Reform Package (2024) — Phase 1 effective May 2025, Phase 2 effective July 2026
- RCD (Registered Community Design): 25 years max (5-year terms). 350 EUR first design
- Unregistered Community Design (UCD): 3 years automatic protection from first disclosure in EU
- 2025 reform: animated designs (.mp4, .gif), 3D objects (.obj, .stl), virtual/digital product designs now protectable
- Repair clause: spare parts can now use design-protected elements for repair purposes

**Patents:**
- European Patent Convention (EPC) — administered by EPO (not EU institution)
- European patent: single application, designate countries. Becomes bundle of national patents after grant
- Unitary Patent: single patent covering 17+ participating EU states. Available since June 2023
- UPC: single court for disputes. Opt-out available for existing European patents until 2030
- Software: not patentable "as such" but patentable if it produces a "further technical effect"

**Trade Secrets:**
- Trade Secrets Directive (EU) 2016/943
- Definition: information that is secret, has commercial value, and subject to reasonable steps to keep it secret
- Remedies: injunctions, damages, corrective measures, seizure

**GI & Craft Products:**
- GI Regulation for agricultural products (Regulation (EU) 2024/1143)
- CIGIR: GI protection for craft and industrial products (applicable since December 2025)

### 2026 EU IP Developments
- EU AI Act: most provisions apply from August 2026. Transparency obligations for AI-generated content
- Design reform Phase 2 (July 2026): additional procedural changes
- Trade mark guidelines updates to reflect AI-generated signs
- SEP (Standard Essential Patents) Regulation: proposed framework for FRAND licensing

## United Kingdom

### Governing Body
- **UKIPO** (UK Intellectual Property Office, Newport)

### Key Statutes
| Area | Statute | Key Provisions |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Copyright | Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 (CDPA) | Duration: life+70. Fair dealing. Computer-generated works (s.9(3)) |
| Trademark | Trade Marks Act 1994 | 10-year terms. Use requirement (5 years). Similar to EU regime (diverging post-Brexit) |
| Patent | Patents Act 1977 | 20 years. Supplementary protection certificates for pharma |
| Design | Registered Designs Act 1949 + CDPA Part III | Registered: 25 years. Unregistered (UK): 15 years (design right) or 3 years (supplementary UDR) |
| Trade Secret | Common law (breach of confidence) | No standalone statute. Equitable remedy |

### Post-Brexit IP
- UK no longer covered by EUTM or RCD. Existing EUTMs auto-cloned to UK registrations
- Madrid and Hague still available (UK member independently)
- No Unitary Patent / UPC participation
- Copyright: diverging from EU on AI policy. CDPA s.9(3) protects "computer-generated works" — unique globally

### UK-Specific Features
- **Computer-generated works:** Author = person who made arrangements for creation. 50-year duration. Under review — Spring 2026 policy proposals expected
- **Supplementary Unregistered Design Right (SUDR):** 3 years, modeled on old EU UCD. Created post-Brexit
- **UK IPO Mediation Service:** Free mediation for trademark/design disputes
- **Enterprise Act 2002:** Criminal penalties for online copyright infringement (streaming)

## Germany

### Key Features
- Largest patent litigation market in EU (~1,800 cases/year at Düsseldorf, Munich, Mannheim)
- **Bifurcation:** Patent validity (Federal Patent Court) and infringement (regional courts) handled separately
- Employee invention law (Arbeitnehmererfindungsgesetz): employer must formally claim employee inventions within 4 months
- Utility model (Gebrauchsmuster): 10 years, no examination (but can be invalidated)
- Copyright: strong moral rights (Urheberpersönlichkeitsrecht), inalienable
- DPMA (German Patent and Trade Mark Office) — national registrations
- Now participating in UPC system

## France

### Key Features
- INPI (Institut National de la Propriété Industrielle) — registrations
- **Droit d'auteur:** Very strong moral rights, perpetual and inalienable (even after death)
- No work-for-hire doctrine: even employee-created software must be explicitly assigned (software exception exists)
- **Action en contrefaçon:** Unified IP infringement procedure (saisie-contrefaçon = seizure-based evidence gathering)
- Strong enforcement: criminal penalties common for counterfeiting
- Paris courts: specialized IP judges
- Punitive damages now available (since 2014 reform), up to "triple damages"
- Competition authority actively enforcing against AI training (Google/Gemini EUR 250M fine)

## Switzerland

### Key Features
- **IPI** (Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property, Bern)
- **WIPO headquarters** located in Geneva
- Not EU member, but extensive bilateral agreements
- Strong trade secret protection (unfair competition law)
- Patents: 20 years, no patent examination (only formal review — use Hague Agreement for international)
- Trademarks: 10-year terms, no use requirement (!)
- Copyright: life+70 years (since 2020 amendment, previously life+50)
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague
- Swiss customs very active in border enforcement (significant for luxury goods/watches)

## Spain, Italy, Netherlands, Nordic Countries

### Spain
- OEPM (Oficina Española de Patentes y Marcas)
- Strong GI protection (food, wine, olive oil)
- Participating in UPC

### Italy
- UIBM (Ufficio Italiano Brevetti e Marchi)
- Strong design protection (fashion industry)
- "Made in Italy" GI framework
- Participating in UPC

### Netherlands
- BOIP (Benelux Office for Intellectual Property) — shared with Belgium, Luxembourg
- Important for holding companies (favorable IP tax regime, under reform)
- UPC Division of the Central Division (The Hague)

### Nordic Countries (Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland)
- Generally EU-aligned (Sweden, Denmark, Finland in EU; Norway in EEA)
- Denmark: proposed AI deepfake legislation (digital identity protection)
- Sweden: strong copyright enforcement (The Pirate Bay legacy)
- Finland: known for patent litigation expertise
