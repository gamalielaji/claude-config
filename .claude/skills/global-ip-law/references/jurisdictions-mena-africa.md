# IP Law — Middle East, North Africa & Africa

## United Arab Emirates

### Governing Body
- **Ministry of Economy** — trademarks, copyright
- **Ministry of Justice** — patent matters

### Key Laws
- Federal Trademark Law (Decree-Law No. 36/2021)
- Patent Law (Federal Law No. 17/2002, amended 2021)
- Copyright Law (Federal Law No. 38/2021)
- Industrial Designs Law (Federal Law No. 17/2002)

### UAE-Specific Features
- **GCC Trademark system:** Regional trademark through GCC Trademark Office (Riyadh) — covers all 6 GCC states, but national filing still more common
- **Sharia considerations:** Marks related to alcohol, pork, gambling may face refusal on public morality grounds
- **Free zones:** Each free zone may have specific IP rules (DIFC, ADGM have common law-based IP frameworks)
- **Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM):** Common law jurisdiction — IP disputes can be adjudicated under common law principles
- **Copyright:** No registration system. Automatic protection. But registration at Ministry of Economy creates evidentiary record
- **Criminal enforcement:** Strong — counterfeiting carries imprisonment and fines
- **Member:** Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid
- **NOT member:** Hague

### DIFC IP Law
- DIFC has its own IP Law (DIFC Law No. 4 of 2019)
- Covers patents, trademarks, copyright, designs, trade secrets
- Common law-based (unusual in the region)
- DIFC Courts handle disputes within the free zone

## Saudi Arabia

### Governing Body
- **SAIP** (Saudi Authority for Intellectual Property, established 2018)

### Key Laws
- Trademarks Law (2002, implementing regulations 2020)
- Patent Law (Law of Patents, Layout Designs of Integrated Circuits, Plant Varieties, and Industrial Designs, 2004)
- Copyright Law (2003)

### Saudi-Specific Features
- **Vision 2030:** IP development is a strategic priority
- **SAIP:** Consolidated all IP functions under one authority (previously scattered across ministries)
- **Sharia considerations:** Similar to UAE — marks for prohibited products face refusal
- **Patent term:** 20 years from filing
- **Trademark:** 10 years, renewable. Use requirement after 5 years (theoretical, rarely enforced)
- **Criminal enforcement:** Penalties up to 1M SAR + 3 years imprisonment for counterfeiting
- **Member:** Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 2024)
- **Entertainment/content IP:** Growing rapidly under Vision 2030 entertainment liberalization

## Egypt

### Key Features
- **ITDA** (Information Technology Industry Development Agency) + Patent Office
- IP Law No. 82 of 2002 (comprehensive IP law)
- Trademark: 10-year terms. Multi-class applications allowed
- Patent: 20 years. Compulsory licensing available
- Copyright: life+50 years
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid
- Largest Arab-speaking market — Arabic-language trademark registrations important

## South Africa

### Governing Body
- **CIPC** (Companies and Intellectual Property Commission)

### Key Laws
- Trade Marks Act 194 of 1993
- Patents Act 57 of 1978
- Copyright Act 98 of 1978
- Designs Act 195 of 1993

### South Africa-Specific Features
- **No substantive patent examination:** Patents granted without examination for novelty/inventive step. Can be invalidated in court
- **Proposed IP policy:** Government has been considering major reforms since 2018, including fair use and compulsory licensing provisions
- **Common law trade mark rights:** Exist alongside registered marks
- **Copyright review:** Copyright Amendment Bill (still pending as of 2025) would introduce fair use and expand exceptions
- **Traditional Knowledge:** Protection of Indigenous Knowledge Bill — long-pending legislation
- **ARIPO membership:** Member of African Regional Intellectual Property Organization
- **Member:** Paris, Berne, PCT
- **NOT member:** Madrid (considering accession), Hague

## Nigeria

### Key Features
- **Trademarks, Patents and Designs Registry** (under Federal Ministry of Industry, Trade and Investment)
- Trade Marks Act (Cap T13), Patents and Designs Act (Cap P2), Copyright Act 2022
- Copyright Act 2022: major reform. Updated for digital age, introduced fair use-like provisions
- Patent: 20 years. No substantive examination (similar to South Africa)
- Trademark: 7 years initial, then 14-year renewals
- Largest economy in Africa — IP registration important for market entry
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT
- NOT member: Madrid (considering), Hague

## Kenya

### Key Features
- **KIPI** (Kenya Industrial Property Institute) — patents, trademarks, designs
- **KECOBO** (Kenya Copyright Board) — copyright
- Industrial Property Act 2001, Trade Marks Act (Cap 506), Copyright Act 2001
- Patent: 20 years. Substantive examination available
- Utility model: 10 years
- Trademark: 10 years, renewable
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid (since 1998)
- ARIPO member
- Regional IP leader in East Africa

## Regional IP Organizations (Africa)

### ARIPO (African Regional Intellectual Property Organization)
- 22 member states (English-speaking Africa)
- **Harare Protocol:** Patents and industrial designs
- **Banjul Protocol:** Trademarks
- **Swakopmund Protocol:** Traditional knowledge, genetic resources, folklore
- Single filing covers designated member states
- Members include: Botswana, Ghana, Kenya, Malawi, Mozambique, Rwanda, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe

### OAPI (Organisation Africaine de la Propriété Intellectuelle)
- 17 member states (French-speaking Africa)
- **Bangui Agreement (revised 2015)**
- Single registration automatically valid in ALL member states (no designation needed)
- Covers: patents, utility models, trademarks, industrial designs, GIs
- Members include: Cameroon, Côte d'Ivoire, Gabon, Guinea, Mali, Senegal, Togo

### AfCFTA (African Continental Free Trade Area)
- Protocol on Intellectual Property Rights under negotiation
- Goal: harmonize IP standards across the continent
- Would create framework for Pan-African IP cooperation

## Israel

### Key Features
- **ILPO** (Israel Patent Office)
- Patents Law 5727-1967, Trade Marks Ordinance (New Version) 5732-1972, Copyright Act 5768-2007, Designs Ordinance 5738-1978
- Strong tech/startup ecosystem — patent activity concentrated in software, biotech, cybersecurity
- Patent: 20 years. Substantive examination. Generally aligns with US/EU standards
- Trademark: 10 years, renewable. First-to-file
- Copyright: life+70 years. No registration system (automatic)
- Trade secrets: Commercial Torts Law 5759-1999
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague
- Active in cross-border IP enforcement

## Turkey

### Key Features
- **TURKPATENT** (Turkish Patent and Trademark Office)
- Industrial Property Law No. 6769 (2016) — comprehensive reform replacing multiple older laws
- Patent: 20 years. Utility model: 10 years
- Trademark: 10 years, renewable. Opposition period: 2 months
- Design: 25 years (5-year terms)
- Strong enforcement framework. Specialized IP courts in Istanbul, Ankara, Izmir
- Member: Paris, Berne, PCT, Madrid, Hague
- EU candidate country — IP regime broadly aligned with EU acquis
